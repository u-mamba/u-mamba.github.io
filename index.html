Overview

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Transformers have been the most popular architectures for medical image segmenta- tion, but both of them have limited ability to handle long-range de- pendencies because of inherent locality or computational complexity. Structured state space sequence models have emerged as general-purpose building blocks for various tasks. Here, we introduce U-Mamba, a general- purpose network for medical image segmentation. The core component is plug-and-play network blocks that combine the advantage of convo- lutional layers and selective structured state space models, which can simultaneously capture local features and aggregate global representa- tions. We conduct extensive experiments on four diverse tasks, includ- ing 3D abdominal organ segmentation in CT and MR images, cell seg- mentation in microscopy images, and instrument segmentation in en- doscopy images. U-Mamba outperforms state-of-the-art CNN-based and transformer-based medical image segmentation networks across all tasks. This opens new avenues for efficient long-range dependency modeling.
